# A custom CRM that fits your team, not the other way around.

> A CRM your team does not use is worthless, whatever it costs. Depending on your situation, I set up or fix your market CRM (Pipedrive, HubSpot), I make your tools talk to each other, or I build a fully custom CRM, hosted in France, whose code you own.

## Three delivery modes: three possible answers, only one right for you

Custom is not always the right answer: sometimes a tune-up is enough. Scoping exists to pick the cheapest mode that actually solves the problem.

### Setup and repair of a market CRM

Pipedrive, HubSpot or Brevo configured around your actual process, or your existing market CRM put back on its feet: a simpler pipeline, fields that matter, native automations, a trained team.

- Pipedrive, HubSpot, Brevo configuration
- Data migration, deduplication, access rights
- Team training on its own pipeline

The right mode when: the tool is right but badly wired: confusing pipeline, empty fields, a team re-typing everything in Excel on the side.

### Fully custom CRM (the signature mode)

When your business does not fit the boxes of a generic CRM, the tool gets modeled on your real workflows. That is the path taken for e-Enfance / 3018: an all-in-one, sovereign CRM hosted in France that replaced their market CRM.

- Modeled on your workflows, not on a template
- Multichannel and realtime where your work needs it
- Hosted in France or the EU, sensitive data handled
- No per-seat licence, you own the code

The right mode when: your work is specific: associations, SMEs with atypical processes, sensitive data, teams growing without wanting to pay per seat.

### Integrations and automations around your CRM

Your CRM is fine, but everything around it is manual. I connect billing, support, email and enrichment with n8n workflows: records create and complete themselves, and the right people get notified at the right time.

- n8n sync between CRM and third-party tools
- Automatic record enrichment
- Targeted Slack or email notifications
- No more double data entry

The right mode when: the data already exists somewhere, and someone copies it into the CRM by hand every single day.

The right mode gets picked during a 30-minute scoping call. If tuning your current tool is enough, I say so and we stop there.

- **GDPR and France / EU hosting.** Hosting in France or the European Union when your data requires it, with data minimization and role-based access designed in from the start.

## FAQ: questions about custom CRM work

### Off-the-shelf CRM or custom CRM: how do I choose?

Always start with the market. If Pipedrive, HubSpot or Brevo covers most of your process, good configuration will cost less than development. Custom is justified when workarounds pile up: business workflows the tool cannot model, missing multichannel intake, sensitive data that must be hosted in France, per-seat licences inflating as the team grows. That is exactly the diagnosis made at e-Enfance before replacing their market CRM.

### How long does a custom CRM take?

Tuning or fixing a market CRM is counted in days. Integrations and automations around an existing CRM take days to weeks depending on the number of connected tools. A fully custom CRM is built over several weeks: first a V1 covering the most critical flow, put in the team's hands, then iterations. The precise timeline is set during the 30-minute scoping call.

### What about my data, where is it hosted?

Wherever you decide. For a custom CRM: in France or the European Union, on servers you hold the keys to. That is the model used for e-Enfance / 3018, which handles particularly sensitive data. For a market CRM, hosting depends on the vendor: if data location matters to you, it becomes a selection criterion from the very first call.

### Do I keep the code of the CRM?

Yes, entirely. Git repository in your name, admin access, clear documentation. No hidden proprietary bricks, no mandatory subscription: you can bring maintenance in-house or hand it to another team at any time. The monthly retainer is a convenience, not a captivity.

### Can you fix an existing market CRM?

Yes. A slow CRM, automations stacked on automations, a confusing pipeline, unreadable access rights: that can be repaired without throwing everything away. And if the audit shows your market CRM is no longer the right tool for you, I say that too, with arguments.

### How much does a custom CRM cost?

Tuning a market CRM or a focused automation is priced in days of work. A fully custom CRM is quoted based on channels, workflows and data requirements. Put that number next to the real cost of a market CRM: per-seat licences that climb every year, add-on modules, an integrator. Custom is paid once, and you own the tool. The quote is fixed after the 30-minute scoping call, with no surprises.

## Off-the-shelf CRM or custom CRM development: how to decide?

The honest rule fits in one sentence: custom is only justified once the market has failed. A well-configured market CRM (Pipedrive, HubSpot, Brevo) remains the best answer for a classic sales pipeline: contacts, deals, follow-ups. The signals that tip the balance toward custom CRM development are recurring: the team keeps parallel spreadsheets because the tool cannot model its real workflow, multichannel intake (phone, chat, email, forms) does not exist natively, the data is too sensitive for a cloud outside the EU, or the per-seat bill becomes a tax on growth. The 30-minute scoping call exists precisely to settle this, and quite often the answer is: keep your tool, let us tune it.

## Why leave your market CRM, and when should you stay?

Stay on your market CRM if the ecosystem serves you: high sales volume, teams already trained, business connectors you cannot do without, a licence budget you accept. Many installations only need a proper repair: rationalized automations, a clean configuration, a well-framed pipeline, and the tool works again. Leaving becomes rational when the real cost (per-seat licences, modules, integrators) durably exceeds the cost of a tool you own, or when your workflows fall outside the frame. That is what happened at e-Enfance / 3018, where I worked on their market CRM before designing the custom CRM that replaced it. An alternative only makes sense when it is decided from inside the system, not from an anti-vendor slogan.
