## What I build: the product your SaaS pile will never be

Four build families come up again and again. Every time, the same principle: start from your real process, ship a usable V1, iterate after real use.

### Marketplace MVP (most requested)

Supply meets demand with payments built in: sign-up on both sides, profiles, matching, commissions or automatic payouts through Stripe Connect.

- Stripe Connect: commissions, payouts
- Seller and buyer onboarding
- Matching, reviews, notifications
- Moderation back-office included

Best for: testing a two-sided business with real paying users, before investing further.

### Vertical B2B SaaS and internal tool

The business application your industry is waiting for: the logic of your process, not a generalist vendor's. Multi-tenant accounts, roles, subscriptions, dashboards.

- Multi-organisation accounts and roles
- Stripe subscriptions and billing
- Import and export of your data
- Business dashboards

Best for: SMEs whose process runs on Excel and five badly connected SaaS, or founders of a niche SaaS.

### Client portal and back-office

Everything your clients or your teams need to see and do, in one place: case tracking, documents, approvals, automatic follow-ups instead of emails and attachments.

- Secure client area
- Approval workflows
- Document generation
- Connected to your tools via n8n

Best for: firms, agencies and service operators who run their clients through email and shared spreadsheets.

### Community platform

Members, content, events, messaging, moderation: your community on your own turf, with your rules, rather than on a Facebook group or a free Slack you do not own.

- Profiles, memberships, payments
- Content and events
- Email and in-app notifications
- Moderation and roles

Best for: associations, clubs, professional networks, paid communities that want to own their audience.

## How a build works: a V1 in production, not a six-month project

The method is MVP-first: we slice the scope down to the version that does the job, ship it to production in 4 to 6 weeks, and iterate on real-world use rather than assumptions. Default stack: Next.js, Supabase, Stripe or Stripe Connect, n8n, Claude API.

- **01 · 30-minute scoping call.** We start from your process and the expected outcome, not a 40-page spec. You leave with a V1 perimeter, a timeline and a written quote.
- **02 · V1 built in 4 to 6 weeks.** Main flow, accounts, payments if needed, back-office. Claude Code as pair-programmer multiplies velocity: you watch the product move forward every week on a staging URL.
- **03 · Production deployment.** Deployment, monitoring, backups, handoff documentation. The product lives with you: Git repository, admin access, exportable data.
- **04 · Iterate after real use.** Real users decide what is missing. We prioritise and ship in small increments, through a monthly retainer if you want a steady rhythm.

## Framework and trust: your product, your keys

A custom application is only worth building if you truly own it and it holds up over time. Here is the framework, with no hollow promise.

- **01 · You own the code.** A Git repository you own, admin access, clear documentation. Everything is transferable to another team whenever you decide, with no hidden lock.
- **02 · GDPR & France / EU hosting.** Hosting in France or in Europe is available, data exportable at any time, GDPR considered from the scoping call onwards.

## Why build an MVP first?

Building an MVP first means refusing the six-month waterfall project that delivers everything at once and gets half of it wrong. A usable V1 in 4 to 6 weeks answers the only question that matters: do real users actually use it? The scope is sliced down to the essential flow (sign-up, main action, payments if needed), shipped to production, then iterated on what real usage reveals rather than what a meeting assumed. This also protects your budget: every iteration is decided on facts, and you can accelerate, pivot or stop at each step. It is the method applied to every build described on this page, from the marketplace MVP to the back-office.

## Custom app or off-the-shelf SaaS?

If an off-the-shelf SaaS covers 100% of your need for a reasonable subscription, buy it: custom development is not justified. Custom becomes rational when you are paying for six subscriptions that each do 80% of the wrong job, glued together with CSV exports, double data entry and rules nobody fully controls. A custom business application encodes your exact process, replaces the subscription pile, and belongs to you: no imposed price increase, no feature removed overnight, no data held hostage. Between the two sits a middle case: if your current tools are fine but do not talk to each other, an [n8n automation](https://mathieuhaye.fr/en/n8n-automation) is often enough, with no app to build. The 30-minute scoping call exists precisely to pick the right option, including when the right answer is to build nothing.
