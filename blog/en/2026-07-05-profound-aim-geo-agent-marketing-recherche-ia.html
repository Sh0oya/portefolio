Profound Aim: AI visibility starts acting

Profound launched Profound Aim on July 2, 2026: an agent that no longer just measures a brand's visibility inside AI, it decides what to do when that visibility moves. GEO shifts from dashboard to automated workflow.

The 30-second version

  • Profound launched Profound Aim on July 2, 2026, an agent that continuously monitors a brand's visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot and Claude, then turns every shift into concrete marketing tasks.
  • The New York company, founded in August 2024, raised $96M in a Series C on February 24, 2026, at a $1bn valuation, in a round led by Lightspeed with Sequoia and Kleiner Perkins.
  • Profound claims more than 700 enterprise customers, including over 10% of the Fortune 500; among them Target, Walmart, Figma, MongoDB, Ramp and U.S. Bank.
  • For some brands, ChatGPT already drives up to 10% of inbound referral traffic, according to Profound; visibility in answer engines becomes a channel to steer, not just to measure.

Profound crossed a line on July 2, 2026. Its new tool no longer just measures how AI talks about a brand: it decides what to do when that conversation changes. This is the moment generative engine optimization (GEO) stops being a dashboard and becomes an automated workflow.

What Profound launched on July 2

On July 2, 2026, Profound unveiled Profound Aim, an "always-on" agent that runs in the background for marketing teams (Adweek). The idea: instead of displaying visibility charts nobody has time to read, the agent watches AI search signals (prompt volume, brand citations, sentiment, answer accuracy, competitive benchmarks) and converts them into action priorities.

It works as a closed loop. When Aim detects a change, say a drop in the brand's citations inside ChatGPT, it looks for the likely causes, writes an explanatory memo, then spins up a "project" with specific tasks. Those tasks are handed to specialized Profound agents for research and content production, with human oversight; finally, the tool measures results and refines its next recommendations (MarTech Series). Profound CEO James Cadwallader sums up the intent: "Marketing teams don't need another dashboard. They need to know what to do next."

Sarah Shaffer, Organic Growth Manager at Plaid and a customer of the tool, describes a concrete result: "The projects Aim surfaces are literally gold. They are perfectly aligned to what we're trying to accomplish." Profound covers the main answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot) and tracks citation frequency, sentiment and competitive positioning on each.

Why measuring your AI visibility is no longer enough

Because measurement alone changes nothing on the invoice. Since 2024, a first generation of GEO tools has been content to show brands how AI cites them. Profound Aim closes the loop between that finding and the action: it doesn't just say "your citations are dropping," it opens the work to push them back up. It is the same shift that, in web analytics, moved teams from the static report to campaign automation.

The economics make the jump logical. Profound, founded in August 2024, became a unicorn in roughly 18 months: its $96M Series C, closed on February 24, 2026 at a $1bn valuation, was led by Lightspeed with Sequoia and Kleiner Perkins (Fortune). The company claims more than 700 customers, including over 10% of the Fortune 500. At that scale, a channel worth up to 10% of inbound traffic for some brands can no longer be steered by hand.

Cadwallader describes the move to AI-mediated search as "arguably the biggest disruption in the history of capitalism." The phrasing is grandiose, but it points to a verifiable fact: when an AI sits between the content and the reader, it is the AI that decides what gets through and how. A brand that fails to watch this layer loses control of its own story, often without knowing it.

The risk of a loop where AI optimizes for AI

Profound Aim's model raises a deeper question. If an agent produces content for the sole purpose of pleasing answer engines, and those engines are themselves AI, you get a loop where one AI writes for another AI. The human reader, supposedly the final target, drops out of the frame. It is the familiar risk of SEO pushed to excess, but faster: production and evaluation are now both automated.

That loop has a virtue and a danger. The virtue: it forces brands to structure clear, factual, verifiable information, exactly what generative engines cite most readily. The danger: a race where every brand publishes ever more AI-calibrated content, until answers fill up with interchangeable phrasings. The human oversight Profound highlights is not a communications detail; it is the only guardrail against marketing that talks to machines while forgetting people.

For a leader, the right question is not "should we automate our AI visibility?" but "how much of the judgment do we keep?". An agent that spots a drop in citations and drafts the brief saves real time. An agent that publishes, unreviewed, the very content that caused the drop, or that optimizes for an engine at the expense of accuracy, creates brand risk. Profound Aim's value therefore lies less in the automation than in the point where the human takes back control.

The "marketing engineer" role takes shape

Profound isn't selling only software; the company is also pushing a new role, the "marketing engineer," backed by training and certification. The idea: AI search marketing is no longer purely editorial work, it is the work of orchestrating flows, data and agents. Whoever can connect a data source, read a visibility signal and trigger an action at the right moment gains the edge over whoever writes blind.

This tracks with the market numbers: according to Fortune, AI already drives up to 10% of revenue for some brands through agents. When a channel reaches that size, it stops being a communications topic and becomes an engineering one, with its dashboards, alert thresholds and automations. Marketing joins the logic that already governs revenue operations and data.

How this connects to my own work

I see this shift up close, from two angles. The first is GEO: I've already written here about why getting cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews is becoming a distinct priority. Profound industrializes what many still do by hand: reading citations, understanding what moved, acting on it. The principle is the same, the scale changes.

The second angle is automation. The loop "detect a signal, prepare a task, execute it, measure" is exactly what I build with n8n. On IA Brew, my automated newsletter running on 93 n8n nodes, most of the work isn't the model call but the orchestration: capturing the right source, filtering, triggering at the right time. Profound Aim applies that logic to AI visibility. For a small business, the lesson is direct: a dashboard is only worth the action it triggers, and that action is better tooled than left to the goodwill of an already-stretched team.

The takeaway

With Profound Aim, visibility in answer engines moves from report to steered channel, with its alerts and automations. The real question for a brand is no longer "do we know how AI cites us?" but "what do we trigger, and how far do we let an agent act without review?".

Frequently asked questions

What is Profound Aim?

Profound Aim is a marketing agent launched on July 2, 2026 by the company Profound. It continuously monitors a brand's visibility in AI answer engines such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini and Copilot, then turns every detected change into concrete projects and tasks, executed by specialized agents under human oversight.

How much has Profound raised?

Profound raised $96M in a Series C round on February 24, 2026, at a $1bn valuation. The round was led by Lightspeed Venture Partners, with participation from Sequoia Capital and Kleiner Perkins. Founded in August 2024, the company reached unicorn status in roughly 18 months.

What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

GEO, or Generative Engine Optimization, is the set of techniques that make a brand visible and citable in the answers of generative engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity or Google AI Overviews. It extends SEO into an era where AI, rather than the classic results page, decides what the user sees.

Why monitor your visibility in ChatGPT?

Because for some brands ChatGPT already drives up to 10% of inbound referral traffic, according to Profound. When an answer engine carries that much weight, a drop in citations translates directly into lost visibility and revenue, which is reason enough to track it as a marketing channel in its own right.
