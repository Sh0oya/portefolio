## What an agent does for you: a slow task that disappears

We do not sell a chatbot to tick a box. Every agent targets a concrete outcome: time handed back to your teams, a faster answer for your customers, a lead handled while you sleep.

### 24/7 customer answers

A conversational agent that answers your customers' questions instantly, at any hour, instead of a 20-minute search through your docs or an email that waits until tomorrow.

- Answers in seconds, not in 20 minutes
- Draws on your real documents (RAG)
- Cites sources, says when it does not know
- Hands off to a human on sensitive cases

Best for: customer support, product FAQ, internal help, cutting the volume of repetitive tickets.

### Lead qualification (most requested)

A bot that sorts and qualifies your inbound requests before your sales team touches them: it asks the right questions, scores the lead, fills the CRM and flags the priorities.

- Qualifies 24/7, even outside office hours
- Writes straight into your CRM
- Surfaces hot leads first
- Your reps only talk to the right contacts

Best for: B2B prospecting, inbound forms, sales teams wasting time on cold leads.

### Internal copilot

A copilot that searches your knowledge base and drafts a first version from your own documents: a summary, a template reply, a case digest, in seconds.

- Synthesises several documents at once
- Drafts something to validate, not to retype
- Stays in your tone and vocabulary
- Saves hours on repetitive tasks

Best for: support, legal, HR, operations: any team that searches and drafts from internal documents.

Your use case is not on the list? That is normal: an agent is built around one precise task. We frame yours in 30 min.

## FAQ: questions I get about agents

### Is a custom AI agent reliable in production?

Yes, provided it is scoped correctly. A reliable agent is bounded: it answers within a defined perimeter, cites its sources when it draws on your documents, and knows how to say it does not know rather than inventing. Guardrails are added (human validation on sensitive cases, readable logs, tests on real questions) before going to production. Reliability comes from the design and from RAG wired properly to your data, not from a model left loose.

### What happens to my data?

Your data stays yours. Hosting in Europe or France is available to remain GDPR-compliant, and the agent is only wired to the sources you authorise. We can restrict what is sent to the models, isolate sensitive data, and sign an NDA before any detailed discussion. You get the code, the access and the documentation: no hidden dependencies.

### How long does it take to build an AI agent?

A first useful agent is often delivered in a few weeks. We start with a prototype focused on one precise use case (answering a typical question, qualifying a lead), quickly testable, then push it to production and expand it. A simple conversational agent can ship in one to two weeks; a copilot wired to a document base takes a bit more, depending on the volume and cleanliness of the data.

### How much does a custom AI agent cost?

The price depends on scope, framed in 30 minutes. An agent focused on one use case is priced as a fixed package; a broader copilot, on quote depending on the sources to connect. On top of that comes the usage cost of the models (pay as you go) and, if you want, a monthly retainer for maintenance. Amounts are set once the need is clear, with no surprises along the way.

### Can the agent plug into my tools?

Yes. A useful agent is connected: it reads and writes in your CRM, your document base, your inbox or your business tools via their APIs. Orchestration often runs through n8n, which keeps the connections readable and editable. The agent can trigger actions (create a record, send an email, notify a team), not just answer, as long as the perimeter is validated during scoping.

### How is this different from a regular chatbot?

A regular chatbot follows pre-written scripts and stalls the moment you step off the path. A custom AI agent understands the request in natural language, retrieves the answer from your real data, and can act in your tools. It does not recite a decision tree: it reasons within a scoped perimeter, cites its sources, and hands off to a human when needed.
