The starting point

3018 is the French national hotline for protecting minors online, run by the e-Enfance association. I first worked on their existing market CRM. Day to day, listeners juggled several channels and scattered information, and the tool reached its limits when cases turned urgent.

What changed

Today, listeners work on Emma, a single workstation designed for them. Every channel (chat, phone, email, WhatsApp, Messenger) lands in one queue. Distress signals are detected automatically and suicide-risk cases rise to the top. Each case is documented in a structured, exportable file, and supervisors follow activity in real time. Everything is hosted in France, on sensitive data, with a full audit trail.

What it proves

Designing and shipping a complete operations platform end to end, on sensitive data. Not just configuring an existing tool.

The starting point

Serious job hunting took hours: sorting offers from three platforms, then tailoring a CV and cover letter for each role. And most CVs are first read by tracking software (ATS) before any human sees them: an overly creative layout sinks an application without anyone noticing.

What changed

The tool pulls offers from the three platforms, scores them on weighted criteria (240 offers analysed so far) and presents everything in a filterable table. For each promising offer, it automatically generates a CV and cover letter tailored to the role, exported as PDFs built to be parsed correctly by tracking software. The sorting that took hours now runs itself; only the decision remains.

What it proves

Data processing is only worth its rigor: if the data is badly structured, the score means nothing. The same standard applies to your business data.

04. Desktop security app

Juice Jacking Guard. USB Threat Monitor (Windows)

The starting point

A USB device is never harmless. Windows trusts whatever it declares: a fake stick posing as a keyboard can type 200 commands in seconds, a rigged cable can steal data. No antivirus catches it in time.

What changed

With Juice Jacking Guard installed, any unknown device is blocked before it can even speak to Windows, then classified across four risk levels. A new keyboard has to prove a human is behind it: a specific key to press that an automated payload can neither read nor guess. Storage devices are scanned read-only on insertion, with an optional reputation check. Everything stays local, with no telemetry.

Taking a niche, half-understood threat and shipping a defence that works, down to the installer and a clean uninstall.

06. Pine Script + backtest

IchimokuSignal Pro

TradingViewSingle GO / WAIT signalBacktest with real costs
Type
Indicator + backtester

v3.3 (Bounce + 15% SL)
Net return (AAOI, 5y)

54% / 1.62 / 24%

The starting point

Most Ichimoku indicators fire too often on long-term charts. I wanted a single GO or WAIT signal for stock picking, validated by a backtest that includes commissions and execution costs.

What changed

The indicator combines ten criteria into one composite score, with a 15% protective stop and safeguards against false signals. A backtester replays the logic candle by candle, round-trip fees and slippage included.

Results (AAOI, 5y)

  • Strategy: +187%, 54% win rate, max drawdown -24%.
  • Buy-and-hold baseline: +1088%, max drawdown -62%.
  • So the strategy loses on absolute return, but cuts the downside risk by more than half.

What it proves

A single-stock backtest is always partly overfit; choosing a strategy is choosing a risk profile.

08. Quant trading

Crypto Trading Bot (MEXC)

Automated tradingPublished results, wins and lossesBuilt-in kill switch
Strategy
Mean reversion, BTC + ETH
Paper P&L
+3.2% over 90 days
Live P&L
-2.1% over 30 days
Win rate
46% (214 trades)

The starting point

Short-term crypto pairs often overshoot their equilibrium. The hypothesis: when two indicators flag an excess at the same time, a snap-back is more likely than a continuation.

What changed

A bot tests that hypothesis in real conditions: a double-confirmation entry filter, capped position sizes, automatic exits, a live results curve and a kill switch. First in simulation, then with a small-scale live pilot.

Results

  • Simulation (90 days): +3.2%.
  • Live pilot (30 days, 10% of simulated size): -2.1%, 46% win rate.
  • The gap comes from fees and execution costs the simulation did not model; fees alone explain roughly 4 points.

What it proves

I also publish what loses. A simulation that ignores friction costs overstates the result; the corrected version is still running, in simulation, until proven otherwise.

Technical note: depending on the project, this work relies on TypeScript, Python, n8n and Claude models.
