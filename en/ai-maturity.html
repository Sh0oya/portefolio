    What is a company's AI maturity?

    AI maturity measures how far a company has built artificial intelligence and automation into its real operations, not just its intentions. A low-maturity small business runs on Excel and manual work; a mature one runs AI agents and workflows in production. Most companies sit between the two, having started without structuring anything. The diagnostic at the top of the page turns that gut feeling into a clear score, based on your tools, your data, your processes and your current use of AI.

    How do I assess my company's AI maturity?

    The simplest way is not to do it blind. The diagnostic at the top of this page places you in under a minute, where a gut-feel self-assessment would mostly tell you what you want to hear. You get a score, a level and your priority levers, with no sign-up. What really counts comes next: the score tells you where you stand, not what to do with your specific situation. That is the whole point of working together, turning that snapshot into automations and AI agents running in your business, instead of one more report that sits in a folder.

    The real cost of staying on Excel and manual work

    Doing nothing is not neutral: every week, hours go into double data entry, into data hunted across ten files and into forgotten follow-ups, while better-equipped competitors answer faster and hold up at scale. The trap, when you try to do it alone, is spreading yourself across ten projects and finishing none, or starting on the wrong one. That is why it begins with a diagnostic that says where the return is, then a partnership that puts the right levers into production, in the right order. Not a tutorial to follow at night: a partner who builds while you run your business. Take the test above to size up the stakes, then see how we work together.

    What are the levels of AI maturity?

    There are three levels, and the test assigns you yours. To activate: lots of Excel and manual work, AI not yet in place, potential everywhere and gains that come fast. In motion: the foundations are there, a few automations are running, what remains is connecting the tools, hardening what is fragile and embedding AI into key processes. Mature: AI agents and advanced orchestration in production, where the game becomes targeted optimisation and custom internal tools. Each level comes with its own diagnostic and tailored recommendations.

    How much time can AI save you?

    For the least-equipped profiles, the first gains are measured in hours per week within the first month: one AI agent, a few workflows and a clean CRM are often enough to remove repetitive tasks. As maturity rises, the gains shift toward reliability and scale, automations that hold up in production and business agents on your critical workflows. The diagnostic estimates where your time is being lost and quantifies the levers to act on first, rather than offering a generic promise.

    Frequently asked questions

    Is the AI maturity diagnostic really free?

    Yes. You pick the quick version (6 questions) or the complete one (20 questions) and you immediately get a score, a level and your priority levers, with no sign-up. The detailed analysis of your answers, with 2-3 concrete quick wins, is sent to you by email if you ask for it.

    What is the difference between AI maturity and digital maturity?

    Digital maturity covers your whole stack and your digital habits; AI maturity focuses on what artificial intelligence and automation concretely change in your work. A company can be well equipped on the SaaS side yet immature on AI, because its processes stay manual and its data goes unused.

    Do you need a tech team to get started with AI?

    No. Many small businesses start with nobody in-house: that is exactly the role of a freelance builder, to scope, build and ship the first automations and the first AI agent. The diagnostic takes your current technical capacity into account so it proposes realistic levers, not ones out of reach.

    Which use case gives a small business the best return on AI?

    It depends entirely on where you lose time today, and it is rarely where you think. The right starting point is not a checklist: it is the one that, for you, has the best return for the least effort. The diagnostic at the top of the page exists precisely to spot it, and the partnership to put it into production. Aiming right the first time is what separates an immediate gain from a project that stalls.
